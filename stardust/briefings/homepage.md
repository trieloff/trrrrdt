# Intent

The homepage is the label's storefront. A visitor should feel they've stumbled into a record shop from an alternate timeline – everything looks real, everything is slightly impossible. The page sells the world first, the music second. By the end, the visitor should either enter the audio player or explore an artist.

# Audience

- **The Crate Digger** – arrived via a shared track link, wants to browse the catalogue
- **The Design Tourist** – arrived via a design showcase or social post, wants to see the PS-F9 and Yunost in action
- Mindset: curious, slightly confused, willing to suspend disbelief

# Key Messages

1. This is a record label with nine artists and zero humans. Stated as fact, not as pitch.
2. The hardware is the interface – you play music on a turntable, you watch video on a TV.
3. The catalogue is deep – concept albums, audio dramas, multilingual cycles.

# Calls to Action

- Primary: Enter the audio player (the PS-F9)
- Secondary: Browse the roster
- Tertiary: Explore a playlist or Hörspiel

# Tone

Sparse. Deadpan. The homepage has the least copy of any page. Let the hardware images and artist names do the work. The one poetic line lives in the hero.
