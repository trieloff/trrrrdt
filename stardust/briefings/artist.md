# Intent

Each of the nine personas gets a detail page. This is the dossier – the place where the world-building lives in full density. A visitor should leave believing this person exists. The page sells the artist's catalogue and links into the player.

# Audience

- Listeners who clicked an artist card on the homepage
- People who arrived via a shared song link and want to know more
- Mindset: curious, ready to go deep

# Key Messages

1. This artist has a distinct philosophical stance, genre, and body of work.
2. The catalogue is real – songs, albums, concept projects.
3. The dossier is the biography. Written as fact. Never breaks character.

# Calls to Action

- Primary: Play all (enters audio player filtered to this artist)
- Secondary: Play a specific track
- Tertiary: Explore a concept project (TITAN, Katabasis, etc.)

# Tone

Denser than the homepage. Dossier-like. The deadpan is still operative but the sentences are longer – this is the artist's file, not a billboard. The one poetic line lives in the artist's motto or philosophical tagline.

# Copy

## Header

- Artist name in Fraunces at their unique axis settings – large, the visual centrepiece
- Genre (Space Mono eyebrow)
- Philosophical operation (one line from the CLAUDE.md table, e.g. «Categorical refusal – identity as given»)
- Channel stripe colour as accent

## Dossier

- Full artist biography from the README.md in their songs directory
- Presented as prose, not bullet points
- Formatted with equipment-panel section dividers

## Discography

- Grid of song cards – title, style prompt, duration
- Grouped by concept project if applicable (TITAN tracks together, Katabasis together, standalone singles below)
- Each card links to an article page for the song AND has a play button entering the audio player

## Concept Projects

- If the artist participates in TITAN, Katabasis, or other cross-artist projects, show them as cassette-label cards with a note about the artist's contribution

# Imagery

## Header
- No portrait. The name in Fraunces IS the portrait.
- Background: artist's environment colour from the audio player palette, with texture/grain

## Discography
- No album art for individual songs (unless it exists). The card is typographic.
- Concept project cards use the cassette-label motif.
