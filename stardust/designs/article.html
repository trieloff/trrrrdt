Liner Note
Titanium spent 119 years between its discovery as an oxide and its first reduction to pure metal. Sylvaine finds this patience admirable. She recorded the song in 1964 and 2031 simultaneously. The Farfisa is from the former. The arpeggiator is from the latter. Neither apologises for being difficult to find.

Production Notes

Sylvaine's contribution to the TITAN cycle takes the element literally. Titanium – atomic number 47, named for the Titans of Greek mythology – spent 119 years between its discovery as an oxide and its first reduction to pure metal. She finds this patience admirable. She finds the metaphor obvious. She records it anyway.

«On ne s'excuse jamais d'être difficile à trouver.» One does not apologise for being difficult to find.

The arrangement is pure Sylvaine: Farfisa organ from 1964 processed through a synth arpeggiator that will not be manufactured until 2031. The temporal anomaly is not the subject of the song. It is the production method.
